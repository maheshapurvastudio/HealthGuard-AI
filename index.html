First-Aid Guide

Emergency instructions. For severe cases, call emergency services immediately.

Choking
  • Ask "Are you choking?" If they nod but can't speak, act immediately.
  • Stand behind the person, wrap your arms around their waist.
  • Make a fist with one hand and place it just above their navel.
  • Grasp your fist with your other hand.
  • Perform 5 quick, upward abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
  • Alternate with 5 back blows until the object is dislodged.
Severe Bleeding
  • Ensure the area is safe. Wear gloves if possible.
  • Cover the wound with a clean cloth or sterile dressing.
  • Apply firm, direct pressure securely with both hands.
  • If blood soaks through, do NOT remove the cloth; add another on top.
  • If possible, elevate the injured area above the heart.
Burns
  • Cool the burn immediately under cool (not cold) running water for at least 10-15 minutes.
  • Remove tight items, such as rings, from the burned area before it swells.
  • Do NOT apply ice, butter, ointments, or break blisters.
  • Cover the burn loosely with a sterile, non-fluffy dressing or cling film.
  • Take over-the-counter pain relievers if needed.
Fainting
  • Position the person on their back. If there are no injuries, elevate their legs above heart level.
  • Loosen tight clothing, belts, or collars.
  • Ensure they have plenty of fresh air.
  • Do NOT pour water on their face or force them to drink.
  • If the person doesn't regain consciousness within 1 minute, call emergency services and begin CPR if necessary.
